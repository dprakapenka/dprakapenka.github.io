About

I am a computational biologist with a strong background in developing and optimizing computing pipelines, tools, and methods for working with genomic data. My research focus has primarily revolved around genomic prediction, haplotype analysis, and quantitative trait estimation in various species, including Holstein cattle, pigs, wheat, and human phenotypes.

Experience

As a Postdoctoral Associate at the University of Minnesota, I have successfully integrated complex epistasis effects in genomic prediction software and conducted large-scale haplotype analysis. I hold a Ph.D. in Bioinformatics and Computational Biology.
